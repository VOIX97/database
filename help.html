Drug-Resistant Strains

Ribosomal Protein Database 


This is a database containing information on ribosomal proteins of drug-resistant strains.


About DRSRPD (Drug Resistant Strains Riosomal Protein Database):  

  • A drug-resistant strain refers to a strain that develops resistance to a corresponding antibiotic after a long-term antibiotic selection. With the widespread use of antibiotics, drug-resistant strains have become the most common pathogens causing clinical infections. Ribosome proteins are a collective term for all proteins involved in the formation of ribosomes. However, no database currently contains ribosomal protein information for drug-resistant strains.

  • To address this issue, we constructed Drug Resistant Strains Riosomal Protein Database(DRSRPD), which is specifically designed for archiving and analyzing drug resistant strains riosomal protein data. These data come from five existing databases, CARD, ARDB, NCBI, UNIPROT, and ATCC. By now, there are 1630 strains with all of their ribosomal protein informations. Additionally, about 10 high-impact publications about drug resistant strains riosomal protein have also been included in DRSRPD.


Protein

This module contains all ribosomal protein information for 1,630 resistant strains. The ribosomal protein information can be viewed by selecting the strain that you want to query. You can download their csv files to learn more details.

Strains

This module contains all the strains contained in the DRSRPD database and their detailed links on Wikipedia.

Upload

This module allows you to upload a database that is not included in the database to update the database. Includes the name of the resistant strain, the number and details of the ribosomal protein.
